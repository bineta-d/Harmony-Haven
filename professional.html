Professional Development
In Student Athletes

The transition into the real world poses unique challenges for student athletes, as they shift from a structured environment where decisions are often dictated by coaches and schedules to the autonomy of making their own life choices. One notable obstacle stems from the demanding and mandatory summer training schedules, leaving little room for essential professional development opportunities that non-athletes may engage in, such as internships and conferences. Unlike their peers, student athletes may miss out on crucial industry experience during these formative years, hindering their ability to stand out in the competitive job market. Recognizing the significance of internships for a smoother transition into the workforce and a more streamlined job search, it becomes imperative for student athletes to explore alternative ways to bolster their resumes. The resources below offer valuable insights and strategies for student athletes seeking to distinguish themselves within their respective fields, even when full-time summer internships may not be feasible.
